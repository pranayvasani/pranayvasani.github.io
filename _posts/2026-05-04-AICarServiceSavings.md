# How I Used AI to Save ₹14,000+ at My Car's Annual Service

Scheduled car services have always felt like a bit of a black box. You drop the car, collect an estimate, and unless you're a mechanic yourself, most line items are taken on faith. That worked fine for me across my previous services — until this time, when I decided to actually dig in.

I uploaded my current service estimate to Claude along with bills from my last two services, and asked it to compare them properly. What followed was one of the more useful 20-minute conversations I've had.

---

### Patterns You Only See Side by Side

Laying three years of bills next to each other revealed something interesting. The core mechanical items — inspection labour, engine oil, filter, flush — had grown steadily and reasonably. But a cluster of detailing add-ons had quietly grown across three services. Not in one suspicious jump, but incrementally — one new line item per visit, each individually small enough to seem unremarkable. You'd never notice unless you compared all three together.

---

### The AC Insight That Surprised Me

One item stood out — an air freshener treatment had more than doubled in price compared to the same service two years ago.

Claude flagged something I genuinely hadn't thought about: my car has a single-zone AC system. The rear vents are extensions of the front blower, not an independent unit. Billing them as two separate zones requiring separate treatment was, at best, a generous interpretation. That single observation saved a significant chunk immediately.

---

### The Outcome

Three calm, specific conversations with the service advisor later — questions, not complaints — the bill came down by **₹14,600**.

The genuine mechanical service was always fairly priced and I paid it without hesitation. What changed was simply having the vocabulary and the data to distinguish what's essential from what's optional.

An informed customer asks better questions. Turns out getting there takes about 20 minutes and a phone.

---

*The car's been serviced. It runs great. And the ₹14,600 is staying where it belongs.*
